The Science of Hydration

Proper hydration is the cornerstone of metabolic health. Every cell, tissue, and organ in the human body requires water to function correctly. From temperature regulation to joint lubrication and waste removal, water is the primary catalyst for life. But how much water should you drink? The old "8 glasses a day" rule is outdated and fails to account for individual biological needs.

How Our Algorithm Works

The Alpotus Hydration Engine uses a base metabolic rate of 33ml of water per kilogram of body mass. However, the calculation doesn't stop there. We integrate additional physiological stressors:

  • Physical Exertion: For every 30 minutes of moderate activity, your body loses roughly 350ml of fluid through perspiration.
  • Climate Dynamics: High temperatures and humidity levels increase the rate of thermoregulation, requiring an additional 300ml to 900ml per day.
  • Maternal Health: Pregnancy and breastfeeding significantly increase fluid volume requirements to support fetal growth and milk production.

Benefits of Staying Hydrated

Maintaining the water levels calculated by our tool can lead to immediate physiological improvements:

  • Enhanced Cognitive Function: Even 1% dehydration can impair concentration and memory.
  • Weight Management: Water increases satiety and boosts metabolic rate.
  • Physical Performance: Proper hydration prevents the decline in endurance and strength during workouts.
  • Skin Health: Hydrated skin maintains elasticity and appears more vibrant.

Expert Tips for Meeting Your Goal

If the Alpotus Water Calculator suggests a higher intake than you are used to, try these professional tips: Carry a reusable bottle, set hourly reminders on your PWA Alpotus app, and eat water-rich foods like cucumber and watermelon.
