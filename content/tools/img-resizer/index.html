Lossy vs. Lossless Compression: A Technical Deep Dive

Understanding the difference between compression algorithms is vital for photographers and web developers alike. Lossy compression (used in JPEG and WebP) removes data that the human eye is less likely to perceive, significantly reducing file size. Lossless compression (PNG), however, preserves every single bit of data. The Alpotus resizer allows you to toggle between these formats based on whether you need surgical clarity or lightning-fast load speeds.

Why WebP is the King of SEO Formats

According to research published by Google Developers, WebP images are 26% smaller in size compared to PNGs and 25-34% smaller than comparable JPEG images. This reduction in byte size translates directly into faster "Largest Contentful Paint" (LCP) times—a core web vital that Google uses to rank your website.
